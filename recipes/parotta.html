PAROTTA

Description

Parotta or Porotta is a beloved layered, flaky flatbread made from Maida or Atta flour. This delectable dish is a staple in Kerala and Tamil areas of Sri Lanka and has spread across other South Indian states. Its popularity has even reached international shores, becoming a common sight in countries like Malaysia and the United Arab Emirates. One of the most famous pairings is Parotta and beef fry, a cherished combination in Kerala.

Parottas are not just confined to restaurant menus; they are a popular street food, served hot and fresh by vendors in bustling markets. They are also a common feature at weddings, religious festivals, and feasts, showcasing their cultural significance. The preparation involves kneading Maida or wheat flour with egg (in some recipes), oil or ghee, and water. The dough is then beaten into thin layers, spiraled into a ball, rolled flat, and pan-fried to perfection. Parottas are typically served with a variety of meat curries, such as chicken, goat, beef, or lamb, enhancing their savory appeal.

Ingredients

  • Maida
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Sugar
  • Eggs
  • Semolina
  • Milk
  • Curd(Yogurt)

Steps

  1. Firstly, in a large mixing bowl take 3 cup maida, 2 tbsp rava, 1 tbsp sugar, 1 tsp salt and 2 tbsp ghee.
  2. Rumble and mix well until the flour turns moist.
  3. Now add water and start to knead the dough.
  4. add water slowly and knead to a smooth and soft dough.
  5. further add 2 tbsp oil, cover and rest for 1 hour.
  6. after 1 hour, punch and knead the dough again.
  7. knead until the dough absorbs all the oil.now pinch a ball sized dough and place in a bowl.add ¼ cup oil, cover and soak for 1 hour.
  8. now take the ball and roll gently. pull and spread as thinly as possible.cut into thin strips using a sharp knife.
  9. bring together the stips and pull slightly.now roll spiral, making sure all the strips are intact.with greased hand, pat and spread the dough.roll slightly thick, making sure the layers are intact.
  10. now place the rolled parotta onto hot tawa. make sure to grease the tawa well.cook on medium flame until the base is cooked.flip over and spread ½ tsp oil.
  11. cook on medium flame until both sides turn golden brown.crush the parotta gently, this helps to separate the layers.finally, enjoy kerala parrota with chana curry.
